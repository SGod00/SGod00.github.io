Introduction to the Internet

The Internet, a revolutionary global network, traces its origins back to the 1960s, with the inception of ARPANET, a project initiated by the United States Department of Defense. Since then, it has evolved into a fundamental tool shaping the modern world.

Key milestones include the creation of the World Wide Web by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989, which propelled the Internet into mainstream use, revolutionizing communication and information sharing. The advent of social media platforms in the 21st century further cemented the Internet's role in fostering global connectivity and collaboration.

Today, the Internet serves as a catalyst for innovation, driving advancements in various fields such as e-commerce, healthcare, and education. Its transformative impact continues to redefine how we live, work, and interact in the digital age.
